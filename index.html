01
Bioelectrical Interfacing
SECTION 01.A

Neural
Transduction

The electrochemical signalling of biological neural tissue constitutes a form of information processing that remains, in many respects, poorly understood. What is increasingly apparent, however, is that this process is not fundamentally incompatible with engineered systems.

Our research concerns the conditions under which bidirectional communication across the biological-synthetic boundary becomes tractable — not as a theoretical possibility, but as an engineering problem with a finite solution space.

The implications of such a capability, if realised at the requisite fidelity and scale, are difficult to overstate.

> Signal class: electrochemical / bioelectrical
> Interface type: bidirectional, high-fidelity
> Constraint: metabolic, spatial, temporal
> Status: research active
02
Adaptive Computation
SECTION 02.A

Substrate-native
Processing

Von Neumann architectures, despite their dominance, impose a set of structural constraints that grow increasingly difficult to reconcile with the computational demands of temporally dynamic, high-dimensional problems.

The brain, which operates under severe metabolic constraints, has arrived at a fundamentally different set of trade-offs. Our work investigates spiking, event-driven, and locally adaptive computational paradigms that can be instantiated in physical hardware.

This is not, at its core, a question of efficiency. It is a question of what computation is capable of becoming.

> Paradigm: spiking / neuromorphic / event-driven
> Comparison point: von Neumann baseline
> Target metric: tractability, not throughput
> Status: theoretical framework active
