01 — The Interface

Where mind
meets
machine

The boundary between biological intelligence and artificial systems is not a wall. It is a membrane — thin, permeable, and far more navigable than most believe.

We are developing methods to communicate across it. Not metaphorically. Not through keyboards or screens. Directly. Precisely. In both directions.

What becomes possible when thought and computation share the same substrate is something we are only beginning to map.

02 — The Architecture

Silicon
that
thinks

Conventional computing is extraordinarily powerful. It is also extraordinarily inefficient — burning millions of watts to approximate what a human brain achieves on 20 watts.

The brain does not process information linearly. It does not clock cycle. It is sparse, asynchronous, and adaptive by design.

We are building hardware and systems that embrace these principles rather than ignore them. The implications extend far beyond energy efficiency.
